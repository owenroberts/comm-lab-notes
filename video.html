    • Background

  • At this point in the semester we have usually already been through two units: Digital narrative and Audio.
  • Digital narrative projects tend to be fiction and look like a comic book. We make them with Photoshop and web development
  • Audio project combine audio recording and editing. These tend to be non-fiction, documentary style audio pieces, like a short NPR story.
  • In these units we cover basic storytelling conventions like plot structure, character, visual transitions and voice.

    • What is a storyboard?

    A story board usually comes after the script in the film making process. In our projects we generally skip straight to the story board which combines text and images and breaks the project down into every shot in the story. It's sort of like a comic book version of the video.

    Each panel in a story board shows the shot, or what characters or scene elements are in the frame, information about the scene and context, and whatever dialog or audio goes with the scene. Ours tend to be relatively informal with a sketched drawing of each scene and some notes. There are varying degrees of information in storyboards, but the general rules is there needs to be one panel for every shot and/or line of dialog.

    You can draw storyboards or download templates and edit them digitally.

    • Getting the raw footage is mostly about organization

    Go through the storyboard and list the locations, characters, scenery and anything else that is needed to shoot the movie.

    Schedule the shots

    Choose appropriate equipment

  • Camera
  • Lighting
  • Audio recording

    • Shooting even the most basic scene can often take an entire day depending on the equipment you are using

    Usually set some kind of time limit, 1 minute, 3 minutes, 5 minutes. Shorter projects generally help with forcing tighter editing and stronger structure.

    Raw footage feedback

  • Is there full "coverage"? Every scene is shot.
  • Footage quality:
    • In focus?
    • Shot composition? (Composition is the place of elements such as actors and props in the camera frame)
    • Well lit?
    • Right equipment for the style?
    • Audio quality?
    • Scenery and acting?
