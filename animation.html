Magic lantern, 17th century

Thaumatrope, 19th century. Demonstrates persistence of vision concept that is responsible for seeing motion in juxtaposed images in animation and film

Zoetrope, 19th century.

Edward Muybridge

In 1880, Muybridge used a device called the zoogyroscope to project his moving images at the California School of Fine Arts, thus beginning the era of motion pictures.

Kineograph patented by John Barnes Linnett in 1868, better known as a flip book. This innovation presented animation as a linear, rather than circular, progression.

Katsudo Shashin, 1907

50 frames, creator unknown.

Emile Cohl, 1908

Photographed negatives of black and white drawings for each frame to emulate popular chalk board drawings of the time.

Winsor McCay, 1914

Early use of keyframes (and dinosaurs), McCay drew frames for beginning and end of a motion and in between frames were filled in by student.

Krazy Kat, 1916. Based on George Herriman comics.

Imokawa Mukuzo Genkanban no Maki, 1917

First commercial anime.

Lottie Reiniger, 1926

Oldest existing animated feature film. Shot frame by frame using silhouettes and tinted lighting over the course of three years.
